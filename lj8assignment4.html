Lamar Jackson was born in florida. Lamar grew up in a poverty stricken family. His father was killed in a car accident.He grew up living in small, uncomfortable apartments and attended different public schools during his elementary years, playing football at all of them. Jackson could impressively throw 20 yards consistently at the age of eight. In high school, he had a good sense of hard work as he although talented, continued to show improvements in his style of passing and intelligence. Apart from football, Jackson also participated in track and field events and even won 2014, Lou Groza, Palm Beach County High School Player of the Year. As a boy, Lamar Jackson was an admirer of Michael Vick.

Lamar Takes college Football

In Louisville College, Jackson established himself as a pivotal part of their success for the period he attended. In his 1st year, he won the Music City Bowl MVP for 2015 after recording amazing feats in touchdowns, interceptions and passing yards. He won Walter Camp Award, Player of the Year, Heisman Trophy award and Maxwell Award as the college football best all-around player of the year in only his sophomore year. his Junior year, he won the Adidas High-Performance Male Athlete of 2017 due to his previous year’s performances. His Heisman Trophy collection makes him the youngest receiver of the award. He won the men’s ACC Athlete of the Year for all conference sports in his final year.

The NFL Calls but at what position?

After the tremendous success that Lamar Jackson had at the Quarterback position for Louisville Lamar was bound for the NFL.During the NFL scouting combine a reporter suggested he didnt have the mental fortitude to play the position and with his tremendous physical gifts he should switch positions and play Widereciever or Runningback.Lamar was hurt by these comments and chose not demenstrate his physical gifts at the combine.His mother who was managing him at the time told the media that her son was a quarterback and a great one and she should know she was there through all of Lamars success and was his main driving force. Even with some suppurt the draft process was a long one and many were uncertain where Lamar would go in the draft

Draft Day

Baltimore Ravens selected Lamar Jackson in the 1st round as the 32nd pick teh final selection of the round . He then committed to the Ravens with a $9,470,000 deal and a signing bonus of $4.97 million.He pledged that day to win a superbowl for Baltimore

ROOKIE YEAR

Jackson debuted in a win against Buffalo Bills as a relief for Joe Flacco and recorded his premiere touchdown against New Orleans Saints. In November, he was named the quarterback starter against Cincinnati Bengals following Joe Flacco’s injury. In that game, he rushed for 117 yards and that stat made it to the Ravens’ franchise record by a quarterback to have that number of rushing yards in a single game. Lamar Jackson helped the Ravens as they won the AFC North title and went on to complete the regular season with a quarterback topping of 5 rushing touchdowns and 695 rushing yards as well as 6 passing touchdowns, 1,201 passing yards and 3 interceptions.During the 2018 postseason, Jackson’s feature in the playoffs makes him the youngest player to start in the quarterback role during the playoffs. After losing the playoffs in a match against the Chargers, Baltimore Ravens decided to build an attacking policy based on Jackson’s passing skills and intelligence.

The Breakout Second Year
In the 2019 regular season, Lamar Jackson had a perfect passer rating and his career’s highest passing yards of 324 in the opening fixture of the season where he also recorded 5 touchdowns in a 49-point difference score-line against Miami Dolphins. He won the 1st week AFC Offensive Player of the Week for his skilful display. He also recorded 2 touchdowns in the following game and set a franchise record for the Ravens as regards touchdown passes. Lamar Jackson set himself as the 1st in the history of the NFL to record above 250 passing yards and 120 rushing yards in a single game. His two perfect passer rating in a season made him the second player in the NFL to do so. He also became the 1st quarterback to score four and above touchdowns in a regular-season more than once. His 86 rushing yards against Houston Texans in the 11th week made him the 1st quarterback to record 60 and above rushing yards in 7 games without interruption. Following the victory against Buffalo Bills, Jackson seconded Michael Vick’s record of 1000 rushing yards in a season. In total, Jackson won 5 AFC Offensive Player of the Week awards and 1 AFC Offensive Player of the Month award during the 2019 regular season. Baltimore Ravens featured in the 2020 Pro Bowl and Jackson became the youngest quarterback starter in the Pro Bowl. Following his 2019 performances, he was named the Most Valuable Player which he won unanimously (following Tom Brady, which was the 1st unanimous winner of the MVP award).
